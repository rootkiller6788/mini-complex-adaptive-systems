# Course Alignment: NK Fitness Landscapes

## Nine-School Curriculum Mapping

### MIT
- **6.045/18.400 Automata, Computability, Complexity**: Complexity landscapes, phase transitions in computation
- **6.841 Advanced Complexity**: Landscape view of problem hardness
- Alignment: NK model as a complexity landscape where K tunes problem difficulty

### Stanford
- **CS254 Computational Complexity**: Worst-case to average-case connections
- Alignment: NK landscapes connect to random constraint satisfaction (random k-SAT phase transitions)

### Berkeley
- **CS172 Computability & Complexity**: NP-completeness landscapes
- **CS278 Computational Complexity**: PCP theorem and hardness of approximation
- Alignment: NK global optimum is NP-hard for K >= 2 (Weinberger 1996)

### CMU
- **15-455/15-855 Complexity Theory**: Algorithmic lens on fitness landscapes
- Alignment: Adaptive walks as approximation algorithms for NP-hard fitness optimization

### Princeton
- **COS 522 Computational Complexity**: Cryptography and one-way functions
- Alignment: NK landscapes as candidate one-way functions (genotype -> fitness is easy, inverse is hard)

### Caltech
- **CS 151 Complexity Theory**: Physics-computation interface
- Alignment: NK landscapes and spin glass correspondence, statistical physics of computation

### Cambridge
- **Part II Complexity Theory**: Classical complexity
- Alignment: Reductions showing NP-hardness of fitness landscape optimization

### Oxford
- **Computational Complexity**: Quantum and classical
- Alignment: Quantum walks on fitness landscapes, Grover search for global optimum

### ETH
- **263-4650 Advanced Complexity**: Logic and computation
- Alignment: Formal verification of landscape properties, Lean formalization
